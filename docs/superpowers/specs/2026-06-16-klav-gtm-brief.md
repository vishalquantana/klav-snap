# Klav Go-To-Market Brief

Klav is a three-product suite (Snap → Sims → OS) built by Quantana. **Snap** is a free, FSL-licensed, self-hostable visual bug reporter (Chrome extension + embeddable SDK) integrating Jira/Linear/GitHub/Plane. **Sims** turns real customer-interview transcripts into grounded synthetic users that react to your product in-product, in verbatim voice — and file any reaction as a bug. This brief recommends a single beachhead, positioning, pricing, and a sequenced GTM motion.

---

## 1. Beachhead ICP

**The profile:** The **Head of Product / founding PM at a Series A–B B2B SaaS company (15–80 people)** that (a) already records customer interviews, (b) has **no dedicated UX researcher**, and (c) already uses Jira/Linear/GitHub — ideally an existing Klav Snap org.

- **Buyer / budget owner:** Head of Product or founder.
- **Champions:** PMs and design/UX leads doing their own research. Research has been democratized — ~70% of UX designers now run their own studies (NN/g, via uxuniversity.io), so the customer-voice buyer is the builder, not a research org.
- **Pains:** The "insights graveyard" — up to **60% of research insights never reach product decisions** (uxmatters.com); teams pay for interviews, store transcripts in Dovetail/Looppanel, and the customer still never shows up at the moment of building.
- **Triggers:** The gap opens **right after Series A** — first PM hired, eng team >7, roadmap handed off, but no researcher yet (openviewpartners.com). The founder stops being the PM and feels "we keep losing touch with customers as we scale."

**Do NOT beachhead enterprise research teams.** They are the most skeptical cohort (64% negative on synthetic users; developmentcorporate.com) and the buyer Simile's $100M is funded to chase. Pursue them only as expansion. Run **agencies — starting with Quantana itself** — as a fast parallel validation channel (dogfood + design partners + real transcripts), not the durable base.

## 2. Positioning & Messaging

**Lead positioning statement:** *"Klav puts your real customers in the room while you build. We turn your actual customer-interview transcripts into Sims — synthetic users who react to your product in their own verbatim words and let your team file any reaction as a bug in Jira, Linear, GitHub, or Plane."*

Claim the category **"continuous voice-of-customer, in your product."** Do NOT lead with the "synthetic users" label — it pre-loads the buyer's strongest objection (only ~8% of researchers trust AI-generated participants; developmentcorporate.com). Use "grounded synthetic users" only as the secondary, research-buyer translation.

**Pillars:**
1. **Real, not imagined (trust):** Every Sim is built from your actual interviews and speaks in verbatim quotes — each reaction links to what a real customer truly said.
2. **In your product, in the moment (magic):** Customers appear as you build and react in their own voice — discovery becomes a live presence, not a quarterly report.
3. **From reaction to resolved (action):** File any reaction as a bug/insight in Jira, Linear, GitHub, or Plane — the same right-click flow your team already trusts in Snap.
4. **Open and yours (moat):** Free, self-hostable core (FSL-1.1); your transcripts never train a shared model — out-opening Synthetic Users via open-core.

**Tagline (recommended):** **"Your customers, in the room."** Backups: *"Real customers. Real reactions. While you build."* and (dev audience) *"Bug reports from the people who'd actually file them."*

**The trust objection — "are these fake opinions?"** Answer it **mechanically, not rhetorically.** Make verbatim-quote provenance a first-class, clickable UI element: every reaction surfaces the exact customer quote + transcript source + date. Reframe trust from *"is the AI right?"* to *"this is what your customer literally said, resurfaced where it matters."* Pre-empt the 80% over-trust fear with honest disclosure: *"Sims resurface and extrapolate from real quotes; validate high-stakes bets with live users."* This converts the category's fatal flaw into Klav's signature proof point. Differentiation: vs Synthetic Users/Simile, grounding is the *default*, not an opt-in RAG add-on (syntheticusers.com).

## 3. Pricing Model

Seat-light, usage-metered, open-core. **Snap is the free/cheap wedge; Sims' AI reactions are the metered revenue engine** — the category proven to support $2–$60/interview economics (syntheticusers.com).

| Tier | Price | Gating dimension | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** (self-host core always free; cloud free tier = funnel) | $0 | Loss-leader wedge | Unlimited Snap reports + all integrations; Sims: 1 persona, 25 AI reactions/mo, 1 transcript; 1 viewer/reporter seat |
| **Starter** | $19 / creator-seat/mo (viewer & reporter seats FREE) | AI volume | Up to 5 Sims, 500 reactions/mo (overage ~$0.20), 10 transcripts/mo, embeddable widget, team sharing |
| **Team** (the money tier; min 3 seats) | $49 / creator-seat/mo annual (~$59 monthly) | **AI volume, not features** | Unlimited Sims, 5,000 pooled reactions/mo (overage ~$0.10), 50 transcripts/mo, push-Sims-to-teammates, persona editor, SSO (Google/MS), API/webhooks |
| **Enterprise** | Custom (anchor $25K–$60K+/yr, credit-bundle like UserTesting) | Compliance + volume | Unlimited; SAML/SCIM, audit logs, retention/data-masking, self-host + SLA, SOC 2, dedicated reaction/transcript commitments |

**Metering dimensions:** (1) **AI reactions/mo** (core billable unit, like Dovetail transcript-hours / Maze responses), (2) **transcripts extracted/mo**, (3) **creator seats** (viewers free, copying Jam.dev). Free/self-host/cloud split: **self-host core is free and uncrippled** — gate compliance (SSO/SAML/SCIM, audit, retention) and meter cloud AI usage, exactly as PostHog/Sentry/GitLab do.

**Rationale:** Visual bug reporters are cheap per-seat with free viewer tiers (Jam $12–14/seat, viewers free; Bird Eats Bug $10–20/user) — so Snap can't be a profit center. Synthetic-user tools price per-interaction and are where the money is. AI cost is tiny: a grounded reaction on Sonnet runs ~$0.005–$0.05 (a transcript extraction $0.10–$1.00) with ~90% cache discounts on repeated persona/transcript context — so **bucketed/metered reactions yield 85–95% gross margin** (benchlm.ai). Cache persona+transcript context aggressively.

## 4. GTM Plan

Sequenced **hybrid PLG**: free Snap wedge for acquisition, Sims for expansion, light sales-assist above ~$50K ARR. 58% of B2B SaaS run PLG; even dev-tools add sales-assist past $50K ARR (saasmag.com). The comps prove the wedge: **Marker.io hit 5,000 paying customers / 3M+ issues** on free-widget→paid-team; Jam.dev built thousands of teams off a free MV3 extension (attio.com).

**Highest-leverage channels (3):**
1. **Free Chrome extension + embeddable SDK** — the always-on wedge. The SDK is a *viral loop*: ship a removable-on-paid "Powered by Klav" badge (Calendly/Loom pattern) + a "Filed with Klav" artifact on every ticket — zero CAC.
2. **Founder-led build-in-public → coordinated launch burst.**
3. **Open-source/GitHub + community (DevRel, Lenny's 15k+ Slack, Reddit)**, with **FSL as the trust anchor** — for this ICP, authentic OSS out-performs polished marketing. Quantana's client base runs underneath all three as the design-partner/case-study engine.

**First 90 days — launch the wedge, manufacture trust:**
- Ship Snap to the Chrome Web Store with ONE razor-sharp value prop ("Right-click to file annotated bugs to Jira/Linear/GitHub/Plane"). **Instrument PQL tracking day one** — Marker.io's bottleneck was identifying PQLs, not acquisition.
- ~4 weeks build-in-public on X (post every 2–3 days, build an upvoter/email list), then one coordinated day: **Product Hunt (Tue–Thu, 12:01 AM PST)** + **Show HN** ("Open-source, FSL-licensed, self-hostable right-click bug reporter") + r/webdev/r/SaaS. Lead the HN/Reddit narrative with the FSL/open-core/self-hostable story (fair.io).
- Turn on the viral SDK badge loop. Dogfood 3–5 Quantana client projects on Snap → first case studies and reference logos.

**6–12 months — scale and open the Sims expansion:**
- Layer Snap Cloud team plans; add sales-assist for multi-team accounts.
- Content/SEO engine: comparison pages (vs Jam.dev, Marker.io, Bird Eats Bug, BugHerd) + 4 integration pages + use-case pages, each with genuinely unique value (avoid thin programmatic SEO — Helpful Content risk), feeding traffic into the Web Store.
- **Introduce Sims ONLY as an expansion to existing Snap teams** — never as standalone "synthetic users." Frame: *"watch your real customers react to this build, grounded in their own verbatim quotes,"* with explicit human-in-the-loop. Convert Quantana clients with real transcripts into the first Sims design partners — their transcripts are the exact raw material Sims needs, and their reactions de-risk the broader funnel.

**The wedge logic:** Snap's instant time-to-value + FSL trust + viral SDK earns the audience and logos cheaply; that trusted base + Quantana transcripts are the lowest-friction path into Sims — turning the category's biggest obstacle (synthetic-user distrust) into Klav's moat, because Klav arrives already-trusted and already-grounded.

## 5. Top 5 Risks / Unknowns

1. **Synthetic-user trust ceiling.** Even grounded, Sims may inherit the category's distrust (only ~8% trust AI participants). Mitigant: never lead with "synthetic users"; lead with Snap; make verbatim provenance the dominant UI. *Unknown: does grounding actually move skeptical PMs to paid Sims usage?*
2. **Snap-to-Sims expansion gap.** The thesis assumes Snap users convert to Sims, but bug-filing and customer-research are different jobs-to-be-done. *Unknown: what % of Snap orgs both run interviews AND will adopt in-product reactions?*
3. **Simile's $100M war chest.** Though aimed at enterprise market-research (a different top-down buyer), it could move down-market into the dev/PM in-product space before Klav establishes the category.
4. **Snap commoditization.** Visual bug reporting is converging (Jam, Marker.io, open-source clones like BugReel/Crikket); the free wedge could become a race-to-zero with no pull-through if Sims expansion underperforms. CAC may not be recoverable on Snap alone.
5. **Transcript supply & data trust.** Sims needs real interview transcripts to be valuable, but integrating Dovetail/Looppanel/Grain and earning permission to ingest sensitive customer conversations is unproven. *Unknown: will teams grant Klav access to their rawest customer data, and is the cold-start transcript volume sufficient for compelling personas?*

### Pricing benchmarks for comparable tools + a concrete pricing model recommendation for Klav

Across the three comparable categories the pricing logic is consistent and tells Klav exactly where to anchor. (1) Visual bug reporters (Klav Snap's category) are cheap, per-seat, with low or no free tier: Jam.dev is free + $12/$14 seat with viewer seats free; Bird Eats Bug $10-$20/user/mo; Marker.io $39-$149/mo for bundled seats (3-15) + $4-$6/extra seat; BugHerd $39-$189/mo for 5-50 member buckets. (2) Research/synthetic-user tools (Klav Sims' category) price on usage, not seats, and are where the money is: Synthetic Users charges $2-$60 PER AI interview (+RAG/enrichment uplift); UserTesting sells credit bundles at $12K-$100K+/yr; Maze and Dovetail are per-seat ($29-$200+/seat/mo) with usage caps (responses, transcript hours, channels); Delve AI prices per persona/data-source. (3) Open-core dev tools (PostHog, Sentry, GitLab) keep a generous free/self-host core and monetize cloud via usage metering (events/replays/errors) + gating SSO/SAML, audit logs, retention, and admin/SCIM behind paid/enterprise tiers — not by crippling the core feature. The strategic insight for Klav: price Snap cheap per-seat as the wedge/free funnel, but make Sims' AI reactions/transcript extractions the metered revenue engine (the category proven to support $2-$60/interview economics), while gating self-host SSO and admin behind Team/Enterprise exactly as open-core peers do. AI cost is small relative to price: a transcript extraction or grounded reaction on Sonnet ($3/$15 per 1M tok, Haiku $0.25/$1.25, with 90% cache discounts on the repeated transcript/persona context) costs roughly $0.01-$0.10 per reaction and a few cents-to-dollars per transcript extraction — leaving 90%+ gross margin if reactions are metered or bucketed.

- **Visual bug reporters (Snap's peers) are cheap and per-seat, with viewer/reporter seats often free** — Jam.dev: Free tier (unlimited Jams, basic capture), $12/mo individual, $14/seat/mo teams — crucially Viewer seats are FREE, only Creator/Admin seats are paid. Bird Eats Bug: Starter $10/user/mo (1 integration, unlimited recordings, Web SDK), Premium $20/user/mo, Enterprise custom. This is the price band Klav Snap competes in. Implication: Snap should be a cheap or free wedge, not a profit center.  _(src: https://jam.dev (via G2/SoftwareSuggest); https://birdeatsbug.com/blog/new-pricing-plan; Capterra Bird Eats Bug)_
- **Marker.io and BugHerd bundle seats into flat tiers then charge per extra seat — and gate integrations/replay/SSO** — Marker.io: Starter $39/mo annual (3 seats, +$4/seat), Team $149/mo annual (15 seats, +$6/seat) — Jira sync, session replay, custom metadata, CSV export gated to Team; SSO/SAML, audit logs, data masking gated to Business (custom). No free plan, 15-day trial. BugHerd: Standard $39/mo (5 members), Studio $59 (10), Premium $109 (25), Deluxe $189 (50), extra members $6.60-$8. Pattern: integrations + replay = mid-tier gate; SSO/audit = top-tier gate. Klav should copy this gating ladder for Snap.  _(src: https://marker.io/pricing; https://bugherd.com/pricing (Capterra))_
- **Synthetic-user tools price PER AI INTERACTION, not per seat — this is the proven monetization model for Klav Sims** — Synthetic Users charges ~$2-$60 PER synthetic interview (RAG/your-data enrichment adds an uplift, ~+$5/user in earlier data), positioning vs $80-$120 agency interviews — pure usage-based, 7-day trial, no per-seat. This validates metering Klav's AI reactions/Sim interviews as the core billable unit. Simile (Stanford spinout, AI digital twins replacing focus groups) raised $100M Series A Feb 2026 (Index Ventures, Bain Capital Ventures, backed by Fei-Fei Li and Karpathy) — confirms the synthetic-user category is hot and well-capitalized, so Klav's transcript→persona→live-reaction wedge needs to be differentiated and priced to land fast.  _(src: https://www.syntheticusers.com/pricing; https://techfundingnews.com/100m-for-stanford-spinout-simile-ai-that-simulates-human-decisions/; https://siliconangle.com/2026/02/12/ai-digital-twin-startup-simile-raises-100m-funding/)_
- **Research repos (Dovetail, Maze, UserTesting) are per-seat with usage caps, and enterprise contracts are large — anchor for high-end Klav pricing** — Dovetail: Free; Professional ~$29-$39/user/mo annual (100GB, 25 transcript hours, 5 contributors), Channels (auto-ingestion) ~$50/mo add-on, Enterprise custom — note transcript HOURS are the metered cap, directly analogous to Klav's transcript management. Maze: Free (5 seats, 1 study/mo, 2k responses cap), Starter ~$99/mo, Organization ~$200+/seat/mo, Enterprise custom — metered on responses/testers. UserTesting: no public price, credit-bundle contracts $12K-$100K+/yr (median >$40K), ~$49+/session — shows enterprise willingness to pay big for grounded user insight. Klav's transcript count and AI-reaction volume are the natural metering dimensions, mirroring Dovetail transcript-hours and Maze responses.  _(src: https://www.trustradius.com/products/dovetail-user-research/pricing; https://www.vendr.com/marketplace/maze; https://www.userintuition.ai/reference-guides/usertesting-pricing-vs-user-intuition-2026-comparison/)_
- **Open-core dev tools keep a generous free/self-host core and monetize cloud via usage metering + gating SSO/audit/admin — the exact blueprint for Klav's open-core** — PostHog: free 1M events + 5k replays + 100k errors/mo, then usage-based ($0.00005/event, $0.005/replay), self-host free. Sentry: free Developer (5K errors, 1 user), Team $26/mo, Business $80/mo — same base quotas, Business buys SSO/SAML, audit logs, longer retention, custom dashboards. GitLab: Free (incl. self-managed), Premium $29/user/mo SaaS (~$19.80 self-managed) adds SAML/SCIM SSO, approvals, audit events. Universal pattern: don't cripple the core feature for self-host; gate compliance (SSO/SAML, SCIM, audit logs, retention) and meter cloud AI/usage. Klav should make self-host core free, sell cloud + AI usage + enterprise SSO.  _(src: https://schematichq.com/blog/posthog-pricing; https://costbench.com/software/developer-tools/sentry/; https://costbench.com/software/developer-tools/gitlab/)_
- **AI inference cost per reaction/extraction is small — design metering so gross margin stays 85-95%** — Mid-2026 token prices: Claude Sonnet $3/$15 per 1M in/out, Haiku $0.25/$1.25, Opus 4.8 $5/$25; GPT-5.4 $2.50/$15; all support 1M-token context, and cached reads cut repeated context (transcript + persona) by ~90% to ~$0.50/1M. A grounded Sim reaction (a few thousand tokens of persona+page context cached + short output) costs roughly $0.005-$0.05 on Sonnet, cheaper on Haiku; a full transcript extraction (long input, structured output) costs roughly $0.10-$1.00 depending on length. So pricing reactions in bundles (e.g. 'X AI reactions/mo' per tier) or as $0.10-$0.50 metered units yields very high margin, comparable to Synthetic Users' $2-$60/interview economics. Cache the persona+transcript context aggressively since it repeats across every reaction.  _(src: https://benchlm.ai/llm-pricing; https://www.tldl.io/resources/llm-api-pricing-2026; https://www.morphllm.com/llm-api)_

**Recommendation:** Adopt a seat-light, usage-metered, open-core model. Make Klav Snap the cheap/free wedge and Klav Sims' AI reactions + transcript extractions the metered revenue engine. Concrete tiers:

FREE (self-host core, always free; cloud free tier as funnel): unlimited Snap bug reports + all integrations (Jira/Linear/GitHub/Plane) — Snap is the loss-leader wedge like Jam's free tier. Sims: 1 active Sim persona, 25 AI reactions/mo, 1 transcript. 1 viewer/reporter seat. Open-source self-hostable core fully usable. Goal: viral adoption, like PostHog/Sentry free.

STARTER — $19/creator-seat/mo (viewer & bug-reporter seats FREE, copying Jam): Up to 5 Sims, 500 AI reactions/mo (overage ~$0.20/reaction), 10 transcripts/mo extraction, all bug integrations, embeddable widget, basic team sharing. Targets small product teams.

TEAM — $49/creator-seat/mo (annual; ~$59 monthly), min 3 seats: Unlimited Sims, 5,000 AI reactions/mo pooled (overage ~$0.10/reaction), 50 transcripts/mo, push-Sims-to-teammates, persona editor, insight enrichment over time, SSO (Google/Microsoft), API/webhooks, priority support. This is the money tier — mirrors Marker.io Team / Dovetail Professional, but the gate is AI VOLUME not just features.

ENTERPRISE — custom (anchor $25K-$60K+/yr, credit-bundle like UserTesting): Unlimited everything, SAML/SCIM SSO, audit logs, data retention controls, sensitive-data masking, self-host with support/SLA, dedicated transcript-volume + reaction commitments, SOC 2, account manager. Gate compliance here exactly as Sentry/GitLab/Marker.io do.

Metering dimensions to bill on: (1) AI reactions/mo (the core unit — cheap to produce at $0.005-$0.05, sells like Synthetic Users' $2-$60/interview), (2) transcripts extracted/mo (analogous to Dovetail transcript-hours), (3) creator seats (not viewers). Keep self-host core free and uncrippled; sell cloud convenience + AI usage + enterprise compliance. Aggressively cache persona+transcript context (90% token discount) so reaction COGS stays a few cents and blended gross margin stays 85-95%. Position Snap-free + metered-Sims as the land-and-expand motion: free Snap gets you in the door, Sims AI usage is what you grow and bill on.

### Ideal Customer Profile for Klav Sims

Klav Sims' sharpest ICP is the mid-stage product org (seed to Series B, ~15-150 people, B2B SaaS) where research has been democratized to PMs/designers/founders but there is no dedicated researcher to keep customer voice flowing into daily decisions. The buying wedge is the well-documented "insights graveyard" pain: teams record customer interviews but ~60% of insights never reach product decisions, and existing research repos (Dovetail, Looppanel) store transcripts without making the customer show up at the moment of building. Klav Sims' differentiator — personas GROUNDED in real verbatim transcripts that appear live in-product and react in the customer's actual voice — directly answers the single biggest objection to synthetic users (64% of researchers are negative on them; the #1 fear is fabricated/ungrounded "hallucinated" insight). The recommended beachhead is the Head of Product / founding PM at a Series A-B B2B SaaS company (15-80 people) that already does customer interviews and uses Jira/Linear/GitHub — the same buyer Klav Snap targets — making land-and-expand from Snap to Sims natural. Champions are PMs and design/UX leads; the budget owner is Head of Product or founder. Agencies (Quantana's own world) are a strong secondary/co-beachhead because they buy these tools as billable client-feedback workflows. Enterprise research teams are an expansion ICP, not a beachhead — they are the most skeptical of synthetic users and have the longest sales cycles.
